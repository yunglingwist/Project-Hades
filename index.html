Welcome to the HADES Exhibition

Step forward, mortal wanderer. Beneath the surface of myth and memory lies a world reborn — the digital Underworld. Here, the boundaries between the ancient and the virtual fade, and every artifact tells a story that transcends time itself.

This is the HADES Exhibition: a hybrid realm where archaeology, aesthetics, and interactive art converge. Each object you encounter — from a bronze blade to a god’s marble gaze — carries two lives: one anchored in our history, the other reborn in the mythic game universe of HADES.

Choose your path: follow the Historical Narrative to trace the object’s real origins — sculpted, buried, rediscovered — or descend through the Game Narrative, where these relics breathe again in fire and code.

Your journey can be brief or boundless. Every visit reshapes your understanding of what endures: stone, story, or soul. Scan the sigils, uncover their QR codes, and you may find echoes of yourself in the myths of old.

About HADES

Hades is a god-like rogue-like dungeon crawler developed by Supergiant Games. As the immortal Prince of the Underworld, you'll wield the powers and mythic weapons of Olympus to break free from the clutches of the god of the dead himself. For more details, visit the official Steam page or dive deep into the lore on the Hades Wiki.

What is a "Roguelike"?

A roguelike is a subgenre of RPGs characterized by procedural generation (randomized levels) and permanent death. In Hades (often called a "rogue-lite"), death is not the end. When you die, you restart at the House of Hades, keeping your collected narrative progress and permanent upgrades, making each escape attempt unique yet interconnected.
